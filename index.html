International tourism in Australia, 2025

Australia's international tourism story is not only about how many people arrived. The 2025 International Visitor Survey also shows why visitors came, where they travelled, how long they stayed, and how spending was distributed across the economy.

Source: Tourism Research Australia, International Visitor Survey. Note: Official survey-based estimates for international visitors aged 15 years and over. Author: Eugene Lee. Date: 2026.

2025 Tourism Snapshot

The headline indicators show a growing international tourism market. Visitor trips, nights and spending all increased from 2024, while average spend per trip also rose.

Who Visits Australia?

Visitor origin matters because high-volume markets, long-stay markets and high-spending markets are not always the same. The charts below compare source countries by trips, spending, nights and ranking movement.

Figure 1

Source countries: visitor trips versus spending

Each bubble represents a source country. Bubble size shows visitor nights and colour shows the percentage change in trips from 2024 to 2025.

Takeaway: China generated the largest visitor-night bubble and the highest 2025 spend, while New Zealand brought the most visitor trips.

Sequential bubble size shows nights; orange-grey-blue colour indicates lower to higher trip growth.

Figure 2

Where Australia's international visitors come from

Flow lines show the top source countries by 2025 visitor trips into Australia.

How to read it: Thicker lines indicate more visitor trips. Points mark the source markets used in the flow map.

Only the top source countries are shown to avoid clutter.

Figure 3

Which source markets moved up or down?

Lines connect each country's visitor-trip rank in 2024 and 2025.

Takeaway: The largest markets were relatively stable. The United Kingdom moved ahead of the United States in visitor-trip rank.

A line moving upward means the country improved its rank by visitor trips.

Figure 4

Which visitors stay the longest?

Average nights per trip is calculated from visitor nights divided by visitor trips.

Takeaway: France, Italy, Thailand and India stand out for longer average stays, even though they are not the largest markets by trip volume.

Longer stays can contribute to higher spending even when trip counts are smaller.

Why Visitors Come

Travel purpose helps explain differences in volume, length of stay and spending. Holiday and visiting friends and relatives dominate visitor trips, while education has a major effect on spending.

Figure 5

Why international visitors came to Australia

Share of visitor trips by main travel purpose. Use the selector to compare 2024 and 2025.

Takeaway: Holiday was the largest travel purpose in 2025, followed by visiting friends and relatives.

Small slices are left unlabelled so the donut remains readable.

Figure 6

How travel purposes changed from 2024 to 2025

The distance between the two dots shows the change in visitor trips for each travel purpose.

Takeaway: Holiday added the largest number of visitor trips, while business travel declined slightly.

The chart compares visitor trips in thousands.

Figure 7

What drove the change in visitor spending?

The waterfall chart shows how each travel purpose contributed to the change in spend in Australia from 2024 to 2025.

Main driver: Education made the largest positive contribution to spending growth, followed by holiday travel.

Grey bars show total spending in 2024 and 2025; blue and orange bars show increases and decreases.

Where Visitors Go in Australia

International visitor activity is concentrated in major states and cities, but regional areas still play an important role. The maps and regional charts show how geography shapes the tourism story.

Figure 8

Colour guide: For change views, orange indicates lower or negative change, light grey is near the middle, and blue indicates stronger growth. For value views, darker blue indicates higher activity.

How Visitors Spend

Visitor spending is spread across accommodation, food, shopping, transport, education and activities. The composition changes depending on why visitors came to Australia.

Figure 11

How every $100 of visitor spending is distributed

Each pictogram represents about 1% of major international visitor spending categories in 2025.

Readability note: Icons identify the spending category, while colour groups the same category together. Minor "Other Expenditure" is excluded so the waffle chart stays readable.

Icon key: plane for airfares, house for food/drink/accommodation, pencil for education, shopping bag for shopping, folded map for tours, train for transportation, and music note for entertainment.

Figure 12

Different visitors spend in different ways

The heatmap shows which visitor purposes contribute most to each spending category.

Takeaway: Holiday visitors dominate food, drink and accommodation spending, while education visitors dominate education-related expenditure.

Darker blue cells show higher expenditure.

Final Conclusion

Overall, the visualisation shows that Australia's international tourism recovery is uneven. Some source markets bring high visitor numbers, while others generate longer stays or higher spending. The benefits are also uneven across states, cities, regions, and spending categories.

The main story is that 2025 was a stronger year for international tourism, but growth did not look the same everywhere. Understanding source markets, travel purposes and visitor geography helps explain where tourism value is concentrated and where future opportunities may sit.

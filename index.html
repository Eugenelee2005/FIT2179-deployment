International tourism in Australia

Introduction:

Australia's international tourism is not only about how many visitors arrived. The 2025 International Visitor Survey reveals a more detailed picture of tourism recovery. This visualisation explores where visitors came from, why they travelled, which parts of Australia benefited, how long they stayed, and how their spending flowed through the economy.

Source: Tourism Research Australia, International Visitor Survey. Note: Official survey-based estimates for international visitors aged 15 years and over. Author: Eugene Lee. Date: 2026.

2025 Tourism Snapshot

The headline indicators show a growing international tourism market. Visitor trips, nights and spending all increased from 2024, while average spend per trip also rose.

Final Conclusion

Overall, the visualisation shows that Australia's international tourism recovery is uneven. Some source markets bring high visitor numbers, while others generate longer stays or higher spending. The benefits are also uneven across states, cities, regions, and spending categories.

The main story is that 2025 was a stronger year for international tourism, but growth did not look the same everywhere. Understanding source markets, travel purposes and visitor geography helps explain where tourism value is concentrated and where future opportunities may sit.

International tourism in Australia

Introduction:

Australia's international tourism is not only about how many visitors arrived. The 2025 International Visitor Survey reveals a more detailed picture of tourism recovery. This visualisation explores where visitors came from, why they travelled, which parts of Australia benefited, how long they stayed, and how their spending flowed through the economy.

Source: Tourism Research Australia (2026). International tourism results. Australian Government. Note: Official survey-based estimates for international visitors aged 15 years and over. Author: Eugene Lee. Date: 2026.

2025 Tourism Snapshot

The headline indicators show a growing international tourism market. Visitor trips, nights and spending all increased from 2024, while average spend per trip also rose.

Why Visitors Come

Travel purpose helps explain differences in volume, length of stay and spending. Holiday and visiting friends and relatives dominate visitor trips, while education has a major effect on spending.

Why international visitors came to Australia

How Visitors Spend

Visitor spending is spread across accommodation, food, shopping, transport, education and activities. The composition changes depending on why visitors came to Australia.

How every $100 of visitor spending is distributed

25.7% airfares.

What this shows

Essential travel costs — accommodation, food and airfares — account for the largest share of visitor spending. Education also stands out due to international student-related expenditure.

Why this matters

The largest spending shares are linked to essential travel needs such as accommodation, food and airfares. This shows that international tourism supports core service industries, not only attractions and activities.

Final Conclusion

Overall, the visualisation shows that Australia's international tourism recovery in 2025 was strong but uneven. Visitor trips, nights and spending increased, but the benefits were distributed differently across source markets, travel purposes, states, regions and spending categories.

The main insight is that tourism value depends on more than arrival numbers. Some markets bring high visitor volumes, while others generate longer stays or higher spending. By comparing visitor origins, travel purposes, destinations and spending behaviour, the visualisation highlights where tourism value is concentrated and where future opportunities may exist.
